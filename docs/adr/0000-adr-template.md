# ADR-NNNN: Short title of the decision

- **Status:** Proposed | Accepted | Superseded by [ADR-XXXX](XXXX-….md)
- **Date:** YYYY-MM-DD
- **Deciders:** Project owner

## Context

What is the situation, and what forces are at play? State the problem and the
constraints (technical, licensing, ergonomic) that bound the decision. Keep it
factual — the decision below should read as the obvious consequence of this
context.

## Decision

What we will do, stated in the present/future tense ("We will …"). Be specific
enough that someone could act on it without asking a follow-up question.

## Consequences

What becomes true once this is in place — the good, the neutral, and the costs
we accept. Include follow-on obligations (e.g. "every new crate must …").

## Alternatives considered

The options we did not take, each with a one-line reason for rejecting it. This
is what makes an ADR worth keeping: it records the roads not taken.
